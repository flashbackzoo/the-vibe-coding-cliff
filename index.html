Why this study exists

New tools are enabling more people than ever to build software.

AI tools like Cursor, Lovable, Bolt, v0, Replit have unlocked software development for everyone. Coffee shop owners, tradies, parents on the couch can take problems they understand better than anyone else and start building a solution.

What the tools won't tell you is when your product is ready for real customers, when it can handle real load, and stand up to security pressure without falling over. I'm researching where AI-built products hold up, where they fall apart, and what founders who clear the cliff do differently.

The hunch I'm testing

AI tools are brilliant for getting you a working prototype fast. But a working demo quietly skips the hard part — whether things will hold up in the real world and the foundations are solid enough to build on. These problems tend to surface months later and all at once. Some AI-built startups clear the cliff without falling at all. I want to know what they're doing that the rest aren't.

Who's behind this

A side project, run in my spare time.

I'm a software engineer. For the last 14 years my job has been taking software from "it works" to "it works reliably, securely, and at scale". This has often been in places where getting it wrong has real consequences, like banks and government.

If you've built with AI tools, your experience is what I'm after, doesn't matter if you sailed through or hit the wall hard. Founders who had no trouble are just as useful to hear from as the ones who got stuck.
